\begin{abstract}
    Linear response theory (LRT) is used to predict the response of a system from the information contained in its unperturbed state. Here we develop a computable linear-response framework for noisy network dynamics near stable fixed points. The network is modeled as coupled stochastic variables with intrinsic node dynamics, weighted and directed interactions, and Gaussian white noise. Linearizing the dynamics around the noise-free fixed point yields a Jacobian matrix and a steady-state covariance matrix that satisfy a Lyapunov equation. We formulate steady-state and time-dependent response formulas and they apply to both equilibrium and nonequilibrium steady states generated by asymmetric couplings or heterogeneous noise amplitudes. The central result is acomputable theory for the equal-time covariance response $\langle \delta x_i \delta x_j \rangle$, which directly describes how local perturbations propagate through the fluctuation structure of a noisy network. The validation of the response functions are verified through Langevin simulations for undirected and directed networks.
\end{abstract}
