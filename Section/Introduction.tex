\section{Introduction}
\quad In recent decades, advances in experimental techniques and data acquisition have enabled the recording of high-dimensional time-series data in a wide range of biological, physical, and engineered systems. Also, the network data become more accessible on the internet, which increases the research activityies of complex networks. Many interacting systems in physics, biology, and engineering can be described as network dynamics, where heterogeneous components interact through weighted and possibly directed connections while being subject to stochastic fluctuations. Understanding how network structure shapes collective dynamics remains a central problem, particularly in systems subject to stochastic fluctuations. From the physical perspective, living systems maintained by continuous exchanges of energy, matter, and information with their environment, and therefore they generally operate far from thermodynamic equilibrium, including metabolic processes and molecular motors. The nonequilibrium systems constantly consume energy and produce entropy to the surroundings, where detailed balance is broken by they directed connection topology and the noise sources of each component are heterogeneous.
